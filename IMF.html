Stellar Initial Mass Function

The stellar initial mass function (IMF) describes the distribution of stellar masses at birth. It is a cornerstone of astrophysics, influencing star cluster evolution, galaxy formation, and chemical enrichment of the Universe.

In SPICA, we explore how the IMF varies with environment — such as metallicity, star formation rate, and stellar density — using both observations and theoretical modeling. Our work spans from resolved stellar populations in the Milky Way and nearby galaxies to integrated-light diagnostics at high redshift.

  • We study young clusters with HST, JWST, and ALMA
  • We use spectroscopic surveys to constrain the IMF in early-type galaxies
  • We develop models based on the variable IMF theory (e.g., IGIMF)

We aim to connect scales from individual stars to entire galaxies — from the present day to the early Universe.
